Tech For Good 2026

February 27-28, 2026
University of Chicago

About

We are excited to welcome you to join us at the Tech For Good Conference from February 27-28, 2026, hosted by the University of Chicago's Tech for Society Hub at the Institute of Politics. This conference aims to connect students across the Midwest and beyond to cultivate interest and discussion on technology policy and ethics-related issues.

Panel Topics

The conference will host 5 panels and 1 keynote, each covering a topic from the below. Our speakers range from professionals in industry, nonprofits, and academia. A few of our confirmed speakers include Chinasa T. Okolo (named Time magazine's 100 Most Influential People in AI 2024) and Charlie Bullock (https://law-ai.org/team/charlie-bullock/). The keynote and one of the panels will be hosted on Friday, and the rest of the panels will be on Saturday. The specific times of each panel and details will be shared by the end of January.

  • The Digital Divide: Broadband Access, Digital Literacy, and Community Programs
  • Platform Accountability, Content Moderation, and the Future of Trust & Safety
  • Public Interest Technology: State Innovation, Privacy, and Consumer Protection
  • International coordination for inclusive technology
  • AI Governance and building Democratic AI
  • AI applications & discovery in science
